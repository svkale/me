Myself

Hello. My name is Siddharth Kale. I live in Pune, Maharashtra, India from my childhood. I live with my mother, father, my grandfather and my sister.

As I live in Pune, my mother tongue is Marathi. I did my primary and secondary education from the local schools, named 'Vishwakarma Vidyalaya' and 'Ashok Vidyalaya' respectively. I took my tutions for JEE in Pune itself. After cracking JEE-Advanced, now I am pursuing BTech in Mechanical Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology, Indore.

Though, I have opted for degree in mechanical engineering, I am always eager for acquiring core knowledge in other fields, like electrical, management and Information Technology.

I love singing, playing sitar, harmonium and tabla. I love listening to indian classical music, and I can spend hours listening to it.

Music in my life

I have been fond of music since my childhood. My father plays flute. He taught me to play our National Anthem on harmonium when I was in First grade. I played it on stage in my school on the occasion of 'Independence Day'. My musical journey started from there. Then, my mother asked me to join a class to learn how to play tabla when I was 10. I was not at all interested at that time. My father, in spite of knowing the scenario, told me not to leave it and continue learning.

In 2015, when a Marathi movie remake of an old musical drama, Katyar Kaljat Ghusali (कट्यार काळजात घुसली ) released, I was so amazed by listening such masterpieces, that I started learning all about Indian classical music. I started singing and playing tabla with a lot more enthusiasm. I think that, the first time I watched the movie, was the turning point of my musical life.

After passing 10th Grade, I started learning sitar from Mr. Deepak Desai, who is a disciple of Abdul Halim Jaffer Khan, who learnt to play sitar, according to Indian Guru - Shishya Parampara. He is very happy with my skills in playing with rhythm; thanks to my father, who always encouraged me to continue tabla.
